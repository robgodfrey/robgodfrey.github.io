We have been joined at different times by our District councillor, Parish Councillors, representatives of the County Council, one of the local school librarians and other interested parties. We have distributed and analysed a questionnaire; and sub-committees have been focussing on finance, volunteers and the Business Plan. Recently one member has been shadowing the current librarians and writing a manual for volunteers; and the possibility of becoming a charity is being explored.</p> <p>The Steering Group is committed to running not just a Library Service, but an extended community resource for the benefit of the residents of Wing and surrounding villages, in partnership with the County Council. In particular we wish to retain this facility for those who cannot easily obtain Library services elsewhere and need a place close to home i.e. children and older people.</p> <p>Our Business Plan has now been submitted to the County Council Library Strategy Project Board. Once approved there will be a transition period from January to August 2012, during which time volunteers will work alongside the present librarians and receive training from the County Council to allow the Library to commence as a Community Library, run solely by volunteers, from September 2012. During the transition period policies and procedures will be finalised for health &amp; safety, volunteers, stock, marketing and finance.</p> <p>Once the Community Library is fully established the Steering Group will become the Management Committee with an equal status for all, but members will take on specific responsibilities.</p> <p>Our aims are:</p> <ul> <li>to create and follow a financially sustainable plan for the running of the Library;</li> <li>to maintain the service in the current premises;</li> <li>to encourage and increase membership and usage by Wing residents and those of neighbouring hamlets and villages;</li> <li>to increase the opening hours and expand the stock;</li> <li>to enhance the IT facilities in order to serve more people of all ages offering opportunities for lifelong learning;</li> <li>to establish the Library as an information hub;</li> <li>to provide additional activities - in particular those that will generate income and bring ‘added value’ to the community;</li> <li>to work in collaboration with the existing school libraries to encourage reading and bring enhanced opportunities to young people;</li> <li>to involve residents in running the Library and making decisions about its future direction in order to give a sense of ownership</li> </ul> <p>In conclusion, our mission is:</p> <p><strong>‘To provide a welcoming environment for local people to enjoy books, have easy access to information, develop lifelong learning interests, and meet with each other.’</strong></p> Library Fri, 25 Nov 2011 15:07:28 +0000 Rob Godfrey 176 at http://www.wingvillage.org.uk Tabby Cat Found http://www.wingvillage.org.uk/node/174 <p>Do you own a male Tabby Cat of approximately 4 to 5 years old that has gone missing?
